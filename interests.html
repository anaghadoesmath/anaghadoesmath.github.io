My broad interests are in Computational Complexity Theory. I am interested in studying about the various resources utilized in Computation, and problems that arise therein. I am currently working on some topics in Algebraic Complexity Theory and Geometric Complexity Theory. I like problems with an algebraic flavor to them.

Apart from these, I love studying about randomness in computation. Whether randomness is essential for computation is a core problem in computer science. I am trying to learn more about pseudorandomness, particularly about expanders and pseudorandom generators. I find derandomization absolutely magical.

My ultimate aim would be to marry a few of these ideas together.

If you have a problem you think I might find interesting, hit me up :)

In the past I have worked in Dimensionality Reduction for Deep Learning and Isogeny based Techniques for Post Quantum Cryptography.
